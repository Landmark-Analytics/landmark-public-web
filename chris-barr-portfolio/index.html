Project: AccountChek

While I was working at FormFree I was tasked with redesigning their user-facing AccountChek application. The purpose of AccountChek is to allow a potential home buyer to share their banking details with the mortgage lender, a much easier process than how it was done traditionally. It was written in an older version of Angular and had a very plain design.

I came up with a more modern design that featured some color, the background colors and shapes are an enlarged version of the AccountCheck logo. The design process was a long one with lots of back-and-forth between myself and the stakeholders to make sure it looked good, suited all of our needs for now, and for any future components that were in the planning stages.

Once finalized, I implemented this new design and re-built the application in the latest version of Angular available at that time. Lots of effort was put into making it accessible to users with colorblindness or poor vision, users limited to keyboard-only access, and users that require screen readers. I put a strong emphasis on test-driven-design, writing over 540+ unit tests that passed on multiple web browsers checked with BrowserStack. This application has very strict code quality controls enforced by TypeScript build options and code linting.

In the end this application was 100% redesigned and rebuilt by myself, working for several months with only light guidance from others.

Project: Smoke-Test

While working with Life After Normal I helped create Smoke-Test. The purpose of this application was to allow QA and software testers to more easily manage information and steps for manually testing complex applications. The philosophy is that no matter how many automated tests an application has, manual testing by a real person is still a necessary step to ensure a high-quality application. Test managers could record what exactly a tester would need to do for each unique manual test in an application. Tests could be distributed to multiple people to be run simultaneously and to collect results and feedback in real time.

I designed the original branding for the logo, website, and emails. I created and implemented the design for Smoke-Test, which was built in Angular on top of an ASP MVC backend.

Unfortunately we did not secure any real customers for Smoke-Test and the project was shut down. Below are my design mockups since I was not able to grab actual screenshots when the application was live online.

Project: Mission Togo

In 2010 I was part of a mission trip to Togo, a small country in western Africa. Many teams went at different times searching for a village that needed the most help and eventually one called Gbentchal (pronounced "ben-jaal") was found in a northern area of the country. A fresh water well was drilled, crops were planted, and a school was in the process of being built. When the group I was with went there we mostly held a sports camp for the kids, but my primary role was to be the photographer and videographer documenting everything.

In 2012 we were planning on going back but we needed to raise awareness of what were were doing and raise the funds needed for us to get there and to accomplish what we wanted. To help with these efforts I designed and built a website for the cause. The website provided information, photos, videos, and allowed for donations via PayPal. It was built in PHP on top of Expression Engine.

The graphics, photos, and videos featured on the website were all created by me as well.

Eject Media

Starting in 2006 after I graduated from college I moved to Panama City, Florida to start a small web design company with a friend of mine. We had a diverse clientele, ranging from photographers, businesses, church ministries, and musicians. We partnered with a local recording studio to be able to not only build the websites for their new and featured artists, but also their printed materials like CD covers and posters.

We wanted to allow our clients to have control over the content of their own sites, but without all the complexity that many CMS's of the time had. I built a custom CMS, based on PHP and MySQL, which was added onto every site we built that allowed our clients the most basic features such as adding new posts, editing them, re-ordering them, and uploading photos. Most of our clients were not at all technical people and they appreciated the simplicity that our custom CMS offered them.

Many of these sites are no longer online, and I did not do a great job taking screenshots of them when they were live. All in all we created dozens of works for clients, but I only have so many images to show here.
